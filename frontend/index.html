Background: This study investigates the use and development of Large Language Model(LLM)'s that have been developed for the purpose of influencing and supporting grading systems at the college/university level. If you've ever had your phone auto complete a message when texting, that was basically what an LLM does. LLMs are really powerful pattern recognition programs that use learned patterns to predict the “correct” response. An LLM will try to “complete” the pattern that they see. The primary concern of this case study consists of identifying any ethical issues that may ensue from the deployment of such systems. This involves understanding how LLM's may affect different people in a discriminatory manner, a random manner, or in an unexpected way. The effect LLM's have against the stakeholders of this case will then be evaluated through different ethical frameworks in order to give the audience not a definite answer, but ideas on how standard ethical frameworks might approach any issues provided from the installment of LLM assisted graders.
